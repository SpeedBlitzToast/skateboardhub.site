ABOUT SKATEBOARD

Skateboarding is a popular form of recreation and sport, particularly among youths. It involves riding, standing balanced on a board usually made of a specially designed 7–8-ply maple plywood deck with polyurethane wheels attached to the underside by a pair of skateboarding trucks.

Considered one of the so-called extreme sports, professional skateboarding includes a variety of competitions, such as vertical and street-style events. Vertical skating, also known as “vert”, features aerial acrobatics performed in half-pipes initially built to emulate empty swimming pools also known as bowls(commonly found in skateparks). Street style involves performing tricks in a real or simulated urban environment with stairs, rails, ledges, and other obstacles.

Skateboarders move by pushing with one foot while keeping the other balanced on the board, or by pumping their legs in structures like a bowl or half-pipe. Additionally, a skateboard can be used by standing on the deck while on a downward slope, allowing gravity to propel the board and the rider. If the rider's leading foot is their left foot, they are said to ride "regular"; conversely, if their leading foot is their right foot, they are said to ride "goofy."
