ABOUT SKATEBOARD WHEELS

Most skateboard wheels are made of polyurethane (plastic material, which exists in various forms), a durable and versatile material that offers a good balance of grip and slide.

Skateboard wheels come in various diameters, typically measured in millimeters (mm). Common sizes range from around 49mm to 60mm or more. Smaller wheels are often preferred for technical tricks, while larger wheels are suitable for cruising and transportation. The Durometer measures the hardness of the wheel's urethane. Wheels are rated on a scale, with a higher number indicating harder wheels. Common durometer ratings for skateboard wheels range from 78A (softer) to 101A (harder). Softer wheels provide more grip, while harder wheels offer a smoother slide.

The contact patch is the area of the wheel that makes contact with the ground. Wider contact patches provide more stability.Wheels come in various shapes, including square-edged, round-edged, and conical. The shape can affect how the wheels perform for different styles of skating.Some wheels have a concave shape, meaning the edges are rounded.
