ABOUT SKATEBOARD TRICKS

Skateboard tricks are diverse maneuvers performed on a skateboard, ranging from flips and grinds in urban settings to aerial acrobatics in skateparks.

An ollie is a fundamental skateboard trick where the rider and the skateboard leap into the air without using hands. The ollie is the foundation for many other tricks and is achieved by snapping the tail of the skateboard down while sliding the front foot forward to level the board in mid-air. Mastering the ollie is a key skill for skateboarders as it forms the basis for a variety of more advanced maneuvers and tricks.

A kickflip is a skateboard trick that builds on the ollie. It involves executing an ollie and adding a flicking motion with the front foot to make the skateboard spin or flip beneath the rider. The trick is completed by leveling out the board and landing cleanly. Kickflips showcase a skater's control and style, expanding on the foundational ollie maneuver.

A frontside 180 (fs 180) is a skateboard trick where the rider spins the skateboard 180 degrees while facing forward. It involves an ollie setup, initiating a frontside spin in mid-air, and landing with both feet to complete the maneuver. The fs 180 is a foundational trick that adds rotation and style to a skater's repertoire.
