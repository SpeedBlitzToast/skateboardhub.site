ABOUT SKATEBOARD TRUCKS

The T-shaped part of the truck also known as the hanger is what holds the axle and connects to the deck. And the axle is the metal rod that spans the width of the truck, onto which the wheels are mounted.

Trucks come in various widths, usually measured in inches. The width should match the skateboard deck's width to ensure stability and proper turning.Also the distance from the baseplate to the center of the axle. Lower trucks provide more stability, while higher trucks allow for tighter turns.But usually brands also have their own measurements.

Bushings are made of urethane which are the same materials as the wheels and are placed around the kingpin to control the turning of the trucks. Softer bushings allow for easier turns, while harder bushings provide stability. Speaking about kingpin, Trucks can have a traditional kingpin (TKP) or reverse kingpin (RKP). RKP trucks are typically used for longboards and cruisers, offering a smoother and more stable ride.

Most truck components are made of aluminum, known for its lightweight properties. Properly assembling trucks involves using skateboard hardware, including nuts and bolts, to secure the trucks to the deck. Skaters often experiment with different trucks and setups to find the combination that suits their riding style and preferences.
