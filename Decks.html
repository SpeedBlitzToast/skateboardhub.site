ABOUT SKATEBOARD DECKS

Skateboard decks are usually made from wood, commonly maple. The number of layers, or commonly referred to as plies(ply), typically ranges from 7 to 9. The layers are glued together with the wood grains alternating to enhance strength and durability.

Skateboard decks come in a number of widths, akin to the skater’s palette, with measurements usually measured in inches. From the 7.5" to the 8.5". The right width depends on personal preference, style of skating, and the size of the skater's feet but the most common width is 8”. Unlike widths skateboard deck lengths are usually between 31" and 33". The length can affect stability and maneuverability.

Skateboard decks have a distinct tail and nose. The nose is usually slightly more pointed than the tail. This asymmetry helps skaters orient themselves and perform tricks that involve flipping the board. A sign of a well-made skateboard deck is having a good 'pop,' referring to its ability to rebound when pressure is applied to the tail. This is crucial for executing tricks.

Many skateboard companies produce decks with distinct characteristics. Skaters often have preferences for specific brands based on their performance and design.Professional skateboarders often have signature decks with customized graphics and specifications tailored to their preferences.
